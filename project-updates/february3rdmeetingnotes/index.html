February 3rd Meeting Notes

Project Wiki

Jason and Ivan discussed whether the project wiki should be live. Jason imported all of the project artifacts. Some of those artifacts had not been completed. Jason closed the wiki and gave access to the open data team members.

Among the updates was one concerning the data sets that will be available for Triangle Open Data Day and our readiness to engage our data stakeholders. I submitted a list of the data sets that Jason would like for release 1. Release 1 is the sets of data to be made available for Triang

Open Data Platform


We chosen open data soft and Jason and Anthony worked with City Purchasing to get the right paperwork in place. 

Communications


Les and Beverly had a meeting with Kim Patterson about offloading some of the communications work to Kim. Kim is an independent contractor. I worked with her for a year at Raleigh where she helped put together communications for Open Raleigh.

Our strategy in using Kim is two-fold:
  1. Reduce the workload on Les, Beverly and Jason
  2. Make the brand about Durham rather than the consultant

After talking with Les and Beverly, the scope of the job strategic and includes 

  1. Communications/editorial support
  2. Completing the communications plan
  3. Managing the social media presence for the program
  4. Measuring the reach and impact of our messaging around open data in Durham


Kim would write press releases and major features. Currently, Les, myself and Beverly share these duties. Kim has no affiliation with my company. I would like to schedule a meeting to discuss how we would bring her on board.


Kim's Comments on Communications Plan


Strategic Comms:


I'd like to review the notes/materials from the first communication planning meetings, and then take a look at any communications that have been released thus far. I think the first step is to hatch out our overarching message and key supporting messages for the project. They should align with the project's strategic plan.


Social media/marketing:


I'll need to get a list from Beverly of the City and County's owned social media assets, including any accounts owned by constituents or valued partners. While we want to reach as many folks as possible, because we represent a public entity, we want to keep the messaging consistent, appropriate, and positive on any city/county owned or affiliated channels. I'd like to start following those accounts (particularly on Twitter) to get a feel for what people are chatting about and where our audience gathers in the social realm. Listening is the key to reaching them, I think. We definitely need to set up a Twitter account for the project if we don't have one already. I'll also need to take a look at the tools we're using to track performance data, analytics, etc.

Events:


Triangle Open Data: Let's hatch out a mini-comms plan for the event, just a short schedule of sketching out what we want to release, tweet, etc.
